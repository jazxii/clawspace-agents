### 1. First EU court ruling under an EAA transposition — Auchan wins on a revenue threshold, NOT on accessibility (2026-05-05)

- **Source**: [Silktide — The first EAA court ruling went to the defendant. Don't read it as good news (2026-06-09)](https://silktide.com/blog/eaa-auchan-court-ruling/) | [UX Planet — The EAA, one year on (2026-06-03)](https://uxplanet.org/eaa-enforcement-one-year-on-dcebf10d4d9c)
- **Summary**: On 2026-05-05 the Tribunal judiciaire de Lille became the first court anywhere in the EU to rule on a case brought under a national transposition of the European Accessibility Act. French disability associations had filed emergency injunction proceedings (assignation en référé) in Nov 2025 against Auchan after it missed the deadline; case heard 2026-03-03. Auchan **won** — but on a jurisdictional threshold, not on accessibility. The court read France's 2005 domestic law (Loi 2005-102, €250M revenue threshold) and the EAA transposition (€2M microenterprise threshold) as overlapping rather than additive, applied the higher €250M threshold, and found Auchan E-Commerce (€144M revenue 2024) below it. Case dismissed. Critically, the court **explicitly described the site's accessibility as "fairly low," with strong or major failures across 13 of the 19 audited sections** — it did not find the site accessible, only that one French law was too small to apply. Associations appealed to the Cour d'appel de Douai two days later; that appeal will set the frame for the whole EU since it is the only EAA case to reach a court anywhere.
- **Why it matters**: This is the single biggest enforcement development since the EAA went enforceable (2025-06-28) and directly updates the open thread from the 2026-05-08 note (EAA enforcement). The headline ("Auchan wins") is widely misread; the citable nuance — a court documenting major WCAG failures while dismissing on a revenue technicality — is exactly the kind of contrarian, sourced angle that fits lane 1 content. The €250M-vs-€2M threshold overlap is a real, currently-unresolved legal question for EU-facing teams shipping AI-generated UIs.

### 2. First-year EAA enforcement map — fines and proceedings are real, but driven by advocates/competitors more than regulators (2026-05 / 2026-06)

- **Source**: [DisabilityWorld — EAA First Year: Enforcement and Penalties, EU 27 (2026-05-22)](https://www.disabilityworld.org/articles/eaa-first-year-enforcement-report/) | [Webply — Real EAA enforcement in 2026: every fine, lawsuit, warning verified (2026-05-23)](https://webply.io/blog/eaa-enforcement-actions-2026) | [Taylor Wessing — The EAA Takes Shape (2026-03-10)](https://www.taylorwessing.com/en/insights-and-events/insights/2026/03/the-eaa-takes-shape)
- **Summary**: Verified first-year actions: (a) Germany — Abmahnung (private warning-letter) wave from Aug 2025 using competition law, not the BFSG directly; first publicly-reported BFSG penalty issued Q1 2026 against a mid-sized fashion e-commerce operator (upper-five-figure range), BAFA as coordinating market-surveillance authority. (b) France — DGCCRF formal-notice tranche landed early 2026 with penalty proposals in the €15,000–€60,000 band; the four-retailer suit (Auchan, Carrefour, E.Leclerc, Picard) still pending bar Auchan's dismissal. (c) Spain — Vueling fined €90,000 (Jan 2026) under Royal Decree 1112/2018 (pre-EAA, mirrors WCAG 2.1 AA) — the most-cited penalty in Europe right now; published-decision regime gives deterrent visibility. (d) Norway — HelsaMi public-health portal under an ongoing €4,500/day daily penalty. (e) Netherlands — ACM closed voluntary reporting 2025-10-15 and began first audits.
- **Why it matters**: Replaces the older, thinner "French groups issued legal notices to 4 grocers" line from the 2026-05-08 note with a verified, dated, multi-country enforcement table. Strong content substance (a timeline post writes itself) and validates the EN 301 549 conformance-checklist-generator idea already in the feed.

### 3. Empirical study: LLMs for automated WCAG conformance testing across 39 SC 2 AA (2026, JSS)

- **Source**: [ScienceDirect — An empirical investigation of large language models for automated web accessibility conformance testing (J. Systems & Software, 2026)](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0950584926002041) | supporting: [arXiv 2605.13873 — LLMs for Web Accessibility: A Systematic Literature Review (38 studies)](https://arxiv.org/html/2605.13873) | [arXiv 2605.27716 — LLM Based Web Accessibility Repair: Detection, Remediation, and Cost](https://arxiv.org/html/2605.27716)
- **Summary**: A 2026 Journal of Systems & Software paper evaluates LLM capability for conformance testing across 39 WCAG 2 AA success criteria. Companion signal: an arXiv systematic literature review (2605.13873) synthesizes 38 peer-reviewed LLM-for-a11y studies and flags that the field operationalizes accessibility inconsistently (varying standards, metrics, validation). The LLM-repair study (2605.27716, Kimi K2.5) reports detection F1 ≈ 0.65 (semantic F1 0.83, syntactic 0.62, layout 0.48), repairs syntactically valid in >99.7% of cases and improving compliance in 80.2% of instances (violations 3.98 → 1.7 per file), but <26% fully resolved and ~30% of patches introduce structural changes. Background figure now firmly cited across the literature: conventional scanners reliably detect only **15–30%** of WCAG criteria (Fischer et al. 2025; Heine 2025) — tighter and better-sourced than the loose "~57% coverage" figure we'd been carrying.
- **Why it matters**: Directly advances the key question "which automated scanners are gaining ground for AI-generated UI" and supplies the precise, citable scanner-coverage floor (15–30%) the domain has lacked. The <26%-fully-resolved + 30%-regression figures are a sharp, honest counter to "AI can auto-fix a11y" hype — a content anchor and a design-rationale point for any auto-remediation tooling.

### 4. Semantic accessibility gap in LLM-generated UIs — LLM judges catch what scanners miss (Politecnico di Torino, 2026-04)

- **Source**: [Calò — Measuring the Semantic Accessibility Gap in LLM-Generated Web UIs (CHI EA 2026, dated 2026-04-17)](https://tommasocalo.github.io/papers/26-semacces-chiea.pdf)
- **Summary**: Analyzes 300 UIs generated by three frontier models (Claude, Gemini, GPT), finding **541 semantic accessibility violations that pass automated checks** (non-descriptive alt text, vague link purposes, heading-content mismatches). Uses LLM judges validated by controlled fault injection (80–92% recall) plus a human annotation study (324 components, 9 UIs, 4 expert annotators). Proposes LLM judges as a reward signal for accessibility-aware development workflows.
- **Why it matters**: Quantifies the exact gap the "cognitive surrender" note (2026-06-02) argued about qualitatively — 541 real violations invisible to scanners across frontier-model output. This is fresh empirical backing for the AI-generated-UI a11y-debt thesis and the alt-text benchmark idea, and pairs naturally with the surrender framing for a strong original post.

### 5. A11yn — first method to align code-LLMs for WCAG-compliant UI generation (arXiv, RealUIReq-300 benchmark)

- **Source**: [arXiv 2510.13914 — A11YN: Aligning LLMs for Accessible Web UI Code Generation](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2510.13914) | related: [arXiv 2602.03850 — WebAccessVL: Violation-Aware VLM for Web Accessibility](https://arxiv.org/html/2602.03850)
- **Summary**: A11yn introduces a reward function that penalizes WCAG violations (severity-weighted via axe-core), trained with GRPO on UIReq-6.8K and evaluated on a new benchmark, **RealUIReq-300** (300 curated real-world UI requests). Lowers the Inaccessibility Rate by **60%** over the base model while preserving semantic fidelity and visual quality. Companion: WebAccessVL (2602.03850) is a violation-conditioned VLM doing checker-in-the-loop HTML repair — 0.211 violations/site, a 96% reduction from raw and 87% better than GPT-5, with a perceptual study confirming visual fidelity.
- **Why it matters**: First evidence that accessibility can be systematically *optimized inside* the model rather than bolted on after generation, and it does so using axe-core as the reward oracle — directly relevant to the alt-text benchmark harness and AI-UI scorecard ideas (both use axe-core as ground truth). RealUIReq-300 is a ready-made, citable benchmark for the WebAccessBench replication idea (2026-05-23 feed entry).

### 6. AI-assisted alt text now rated *higher* than human-only by HCI experts (arXiv 2602.08937, dated 2026-02-10)

- **Source**: [arXiv 2602.08937 — (AI-assisted vs human alt text for HCI figures; 11 expert evaluators)](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2602.08937) | supporting: [ETH Research Collection — Seeing History Unseen: VLMs for WCAG-Compliant Alt-Text in Digital Heritage (Mähr & Twente)](https://www.research-collection.ethz.ch/server/api/core/bitstreams/62f0815b-7b3a-4043-a71b-4b53e7fb1529/content)
- **Summary**: Two studies on alt text for HCI figures; 11 HCI experts evaluated alt text written by DSO (disability service office) professionals vs AI-assisted. AI-assisted (HAI) alt text rated **superior in quality even with minor inaccuracies** — higher on accuracy (U=2670, p=0.016), completeness, and clarity (U=2856.5, p=0.001); human-only text criticized for lacking detail and figure relationships. The ETH heritage-collections study counterweights this: 4 VLMs (Gemini 2.5 Flash Lite, Llama 4 Maverick, GPT-4o mini, Qwen 3 VL 8B) showed no statistically significant quality differences but recurring errors — factual misrecognition, selective omission, uncritical reproduction of harmful historical terminology — concluding VLMs are "operationally viable but epistemically fragile."
- **Why it matters**: Updates the long-standing alt-text open thread ("how well do LLMs generate alt text vs human raters?") with a recent result where AI-assisted *beats* human-only on expert ratings — a reversal of the older "73% of AI alt text wrong" framing (AudioEye 2024 in the 2026-05-08 note). The epistemic-fragility caveat keeps it honest. Together these are the strongest current evidence for the alt-text benchmark harness idea and a nuanced content angle ("AI alt text is now better than humans — with one dangerous caveat").
