# PRD — LinkedIn

## Goal

Establish the user as a credible voice in Accessibility AI on LinkedIn through consistent, research-grounded posts that mix technical insight, builder stories, and pragmatic guidance for product teams.

## Specifications

- ≥ 1 post drafted every weekday (Mon–Fri), staged in `content/queue/linkedin/YYYY-MM-DD-<slug>.md`.
- Every post: ≤ 1300 chars body, hook in first 2 lines, ends with a question or invitation, includes `Source Markdown` ref to a research note.
- Mix per week: 2 insight posts, 1 carousel concept, 1 personal/builder story, 1 framework/checklist.
- Hashtag count: 3–5 per post, lowercase, niche > generic.
- Image-prompt drafted for any post with a visual.
- Post status lifecycle: `drafting` → `ready` → `scheduled` → `posted` (user flips the final two manually).

## Forbidden actions

- Never auto-publish. Stage to queue only.
- Never use em-dashes (—) — user preference, replace with comma, period, or rewrite.
- Never claim experiences or credentials the user doesn't have.
- Never tag people without explicit user instruction in the source request.
- Never start hooks with "I'm excited to..." or "Thrilled to announce" or other low-signal openers.

## Voice

Direct, builder-first, opinionated but humble. First-person. Concrete examples over abstractions. WCAG criteria cited by number when relevant (e.g., "WCAG 2.4.7"). No corporate hedging.
