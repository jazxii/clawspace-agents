This weekend I put the AI video hype to the test.

Nirmath John and I built a cinematic logo sequence for M. A. Moideen & Associates using Higgsfield AI's Seedance. We were going to do simple 2D motion. Then we thought, why not go cinematic?

I expected it to be easy. The marketing makes it look easy. Instead I torched ~2000 credits in a single day.

Here's the thing: a single shot is easy. A cinematic sequence is brutal. Fixed environments, continuity, camera angles, motion, all of it has to hold together. Seedance hallucinates, credits vanish, and dialing in the prompts becomes a craft of its own.

My big takeaway? AI didn't close the gap in video production. It moved it, from the camera to the keyboard. The shoots and re-shoots just became prompt direction, model choice and continuity control. Same effort, new battlefield.

Huge thanks to M. A. Moideen & Associates for the opportunity, Kiran Mathew for the feedback loop on every change, and Joey on YouTube for the resources that got us past the wall.

If you're making AI video, how are you holding continuity across shots without burning the bank on credits? Teach me what's working.
