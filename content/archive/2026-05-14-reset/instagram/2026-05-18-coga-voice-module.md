## Slide 3: Pillar one — predictability

**Headline:** 1. Predictability

**Body:**
Same prompt should produce comparable responses.
Random variation in tone, length, or structure breaks users with attention or memory differences.
Temperature zero is an accessibility setting. Not just a determinism one.
**Image prompt:** "Two chat bubbles with the same input prompt, producing two visibly different responses. A small dial above labeled 'temperature' with an arrow pointing to 0. Minimal flat illustration, deep blue accent, accessible palette, no people."

---

## Slide 4: Pillar two — recoverability

**Headline:** 2. Recoverability

**Body:**
Every voice or chat interaction needs a visible, editable history.
Users who lose track need to scroll back, re-read, correct.
Voice-only flows with no transcript fail this hard.
**Image prompt:** "A chat history panel on the right of a phone mockup, with a finger scrolling up through previous messages. A small edit pencil icon hovers next to one of the messages. Minimal flat illustration, sage green and cream palette, accessible contrast, no people."

---

## Slide 5: Pillar three — plain language

**Headline:** 3. Plain language defaults

**Body:**
LLMs default to a reading age of 14 to 18 in English.
Many users with cognitive disabilities need 9 to 11.
A "simpler language" toggle is a real requirement, not a nice-to-have.
**Image prompt:** "A toggle switch labeled 'Simpler language'. Below it, two text panels side by side. Left panel labeled 'reading age 16' with dense text. Right panel labeled 'reading age 10' with shorter, cleaner text. Minimal flat illustration, accessible palette, no people."

## Slide 6: Pillar four — multimodal

**Headline:** 4. Multimodal access

**Body:**
Voice is one channel. The interface must also support keyboard, focus-visible controls, and screen readers.
Voice-only AI chat is not COGA-compliant.
Build voice. Then make sure everything else still works.
**Image prompt:** "Four small icons in a row: microphone, keyboard, focus ring, screen reader. All four have green check marks. A faded 'voice only' icon below them with a red x. Minimal flat illustration, deep blue and amber accents, accessible palette, no people."
