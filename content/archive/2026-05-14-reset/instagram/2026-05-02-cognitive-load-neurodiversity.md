## Caption (front-loaded for the "...more" cut)

Accessibility isn't just contrast and alt text. Swipe for the cognitive layer most teams forget.

1 in 5 people are neurodivergent. Many more are in temporarily high-load states. Here's what changes when you design for cognitive accessibility, and why it ends up helping everyone.

Save this for your next design review.

## Slide 1: Cover

**Title:** Cognitive accessibility
**Subtitle:** The part of a11y most teams skip
**Visual cue:** Iceberg metaphor. Visible tip is contrast, alt text, focus. Hidden mass is cognitive load.

## Slide 2: Why it matters

**Headline:** 1 in 5

**Body:**
Roughly 15 to 20% of people are neurodivergent.
Many more are in temporary high-load states. Tired, stressed, parenting, learning a second language.
This is your largest under-served accessibility group.

## Slide 3: WCAG 2.2 added 3 cognitive criteria

**Headline:** The new floor

**Body:**
3.2.6 Consistent Help. Same place, every page.
3.3.7 Redundant Entry. Don't re-ask for info.
3.3.8 Accessible Authentication. No cognitive puzzles to log in.

If you haven't checked these, that's where to start.

## Slide 4: What ADHD-friendly design looks like

**Headline:** ADHD

**Body:**
Save-as-you-go on every form.
No artificial countdown timers.
Visible state at every step.
Predictable navigation. Surprise modals are hostile.

## Slide 5: What autism-friendly design looks like

**Headline:** Autism

**Body:**
Honor prefers-reduced-motion.
Plain, literal copy. Skip the idioms.
No autoplay video or sound.
Don't move features around between sessions.

## Slide 6: What dyslexia-friendly design looks like

**Headline:** Dyslexia

**Body:**
Line-height 1.5 or higher.
Line length 45 to 75 characters.
Left-aligned, not justified.
Reading mode and text-to-speech, where you can.

## Slide 7: The curb-cut effect

**Headline:** Built for some, helps everyone

**Body:**
Reduced motion helps autistic users. It also helps everyone in a moving car.
Save-as-you-go helps ADHD users. It also helps everyone with bad Wi-Fi.
Plain language helps cognitive a11y. It also helps every tired user at 4pm.

This is the work. Ship it.
