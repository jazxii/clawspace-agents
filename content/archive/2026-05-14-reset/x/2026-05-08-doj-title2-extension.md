## 1/5

DOJ pushed the ADA Title II deadline back a year.

States and cities over 50k now have until April 26, 2027 to hit WCAG 2.1 AA. Smaller jurisdictions get until 2028.

The standard did not change. Only the clock did.

## 2/5

Half my feed is reading this as a reprieve. It is not.

WCAG 2.1 AA still applies. Lawsuits and DOJ complaints are still on the table. Users on screen readers are not waiting for 2027 to file.

## 3/5

What did not change:

axe-core still catches around 57% of issues. Cognitive a11y still needs human review. Focus traps in custom components still ship in v0 and Bolt output.

A year does not fix any of that on its own.

## 4/5

What I would do with the extra 12 months:

Scanner in CI on day one. Quarterly manual + AT user audit. Document a conformance trajectory, VPAT-shaped, even if you are not procuring.

Build the muscle while pressure is low.

## 5/5

The teams that treat the extension as breathing room will use it.

The teams that treat it as a snooze button will be in the same place in April 2027 they were in this April.

Pick now.
