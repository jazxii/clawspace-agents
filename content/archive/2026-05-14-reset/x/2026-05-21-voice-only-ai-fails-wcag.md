## 1/5

Voice-only AI is not accessible.

I keep seeing demos pitched as "a11y wins" because they replace typing with speech.

That is one user need. It is not the test.

## 2/5

WCAG 2.2 has a clear position.

A flow accessible to speech but not to keyboard, focus-visible navigation, and screen readers fails 2.1.1, 2.4.7, and 4.1.2.

It fails on day one. There is no debate.

## 3/5

Voice as a11y has a real grain of truth.

For users with motor disabilities, dyslexia, or temporary injury, speaking is genuinely easier than typing.

Voice expands access for some. That is not the same as "is accessible."

## 4/5

Who voice-only locks out:

Deaf users. Anyone in a noisy environment. Users with speech disabilities. Non-native pronunciation. Blind keyboard users who still need focus indicators on the surrounding UI.

Voice alone misses all of them.

## 5/5

The fix is not hard. It is just not a magic-moment demo.

Build the voice flow. Add a text input that does the same thing. Keyboard navigable. Visible focus. Readable transcript. Captions on audio replies.

Multimodal is the floor. Voice alone is half the story.
