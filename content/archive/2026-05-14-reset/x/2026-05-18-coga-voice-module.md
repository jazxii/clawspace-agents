## 1/7

W3C COGA published the first cognitive a11y document targeting AI voice and chat interfaces.

If you ship LLM assistants, this is the requirements doc you have been missing.

Released February 5, 2026. Pre-normative. Worth reading now.

## 2/7

Four rules I am pulling out of it for product teams.

## 3/7

One. Predictability.

The same prompt should produce comparable responses. Random variation in tone, length, or structure breaks users with attention or memory differences.

Temperature zero is an a11y setting, not just a determinism one.

## 4/7

Two. Recoverability.

Every voice or chat interaction needs a visible, editable history. Users who lose track need to scroll back, re-read, correct.

Voice-only flows with no transcript fail this hard.

## 5/7

Three. Plain language defaults.

LLMs default to a reading age around 14 to 18 in English. Users with cognitive disabilities often need 9 to 11.

A "simpler language" toggle is a real requirement, not a nice-to-have.

## 6/7

Four. Multimodal access.

Voice is one channel. The interface must also support keyboard, focus-visible controls, and screen reader compatibility.

Voice-only AI chat is not COGA-compliant. Not now. Not on day one.

## 7/7

The cognitive a11y floor for AI chat just moved from "we have not thought about it" to "four named failure categories with research citations."

That is a fast trajectory. Procurement will catch up by 2027.
