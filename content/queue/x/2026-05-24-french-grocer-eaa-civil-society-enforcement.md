## 1

the first four European Accessibility Act (EAA) lawsuits in France are now six weeks past their November 2025 emergency injunctions. Auchan, Carrefour, E. Leclerc, Picard. no court ruling. no fines. and that is the story, not the absence of one.

## 2

the timeline. July 8 2025 legal notices. September 1 2025 deadline for compliance. November 2025 emergency injunctions filed when compliance proved insufficient. May 2026, the cases still sit in court. nine months of pressure, zero penalties.

## 3

the enforcement vector is the interesting part. it is not ARCOM. it is not DGCCRF. it is civil society. disability advocacy groups filed the original notices and filed the injunctions. the French regulators have been a spectator.

## 4

Deque's 2026 analysis confirms the pattern is replicating in Germany and Italy. advocacy groups, not regulators, are the realistic first contact for any EU-facing site that ships AI-generated UIs into production this year.

## 5

most EAA discourse on LinkedIn this quarter has been "what fines are coming?" the fines have not come and the cases are still moving. the operational read is: plan remediation around advocacy-group audits, not regulator action.

## 6

the argument worth having is not whether the EAA has teeth. it is which audit you would survive: a disability-advocacy group testing your checkout flow with a screen reader, or an automated scanner report. those produce different remediation roadmaps.
