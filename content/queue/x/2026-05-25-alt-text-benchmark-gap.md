## 1/5

There is no peer-reviewed benchmark comparing GPT-4o, Claude, and Gemini on alt-text quality.

I have looked. As of May 2026, I cannot find one.

This is strange.

## 2/5

We have benchmarks for everything else.

MMLU. GPQA. SWE-bench. WritingBench. Reasoning, coding, math, writing.

Image description for accessibility is not on the list.

## 3/5

These models are now the default alt-text generator for millions of images per day.

Slack. Notion. Microsoft Office. Apple Photos. Every CMS with an "AI alt text" toggle.

Without a benchmark, the comparison is anecdote.

## 4/5

What the benchmark needs:

Standardised image corpus across categories.
Sighted experts plus blind end-user raters.
Per-category scoring.
Failure mode tagging (hallucination, over-description, wrong tone).
Open data, open methodology.

## 5/5

Sketching the harness in my notes. ASSETS 2026 deadline is open later this year.

If anyone is already running this, I would much rather collaborate than duplicate.

Or is the field really this quiet on it?
