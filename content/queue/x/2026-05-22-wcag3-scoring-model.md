## 1/4

WCAG 3 is not a compliance target. Will not be one for years.

So why are 2026 RFPs already asking for "accessibility maturity level" instead of "WCAG 2.1 AA conformance"?

The framing moved before the standard did.

## 2/4

Three things I am noticing in 2026 procurement language:

RFPs ask for maturity level, not pass/fail.
VPATs are getting longer, with methodology sections.
"WCAG report with 12 violations" is starting to look thin next to scored findings.

## 3/4

If you ship AI a11y tooling that reports a violation count and nothing else, that worked through 2026.

By 2028 the comparison your buyers expect is "12 violations, scoring 2.4 of 5 on perceivable, methodology documented."

## 4/4

What I am not saying: APCA, time-to-completion, or any specific WCAG 3 metric will survive the next draft.

The structure of scoring is what is hardening. The metrics are still in flux.

Build the scoring scaffold now. Slot the metrics in later.
