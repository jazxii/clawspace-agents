WCAG 3.0's March 2026 Working Draft now contains 174 outcomes. WCAG 2.1 had 78 success criteria. The scope more than doubled.

A few things to know before you panic.

The W3C is clear: these are "developing status" outcomes. Many will be cut. The draft is a survey of what could matter, not a final list. Candidate Recommendation is targeted for Q4 2027. Final recommendation, no earlier than 2029.

WCAG 2.2 is not going anywhere when WCAG 3.0 lands. The APCA contrast algorithm is still unresolved. Do not rebuild your conformance program around an outcome that might disappear.

Three new categories caught my attention.

Non-verbal cue description. Media alternatives that capture tone and emotion, not just the literal words. A direct response to AI-generated transcripts that flatten meaning.

Single-idea text segments. A cognitive support outcome that targets memory load in long copy. AI summarization tools could either help or hurt here, depending on how they are tuned.

Disability information privacy. This is the one I keep thinking about. The outcome targets disclosure of a user's disability information to third-party algorithms. If it survives the cut, it may force a hard conversation with overlay tools and personalization vendors that route accessibility preferences through external services.

WCAG 3.0 is years out. But the direction is clear. More outcomes, more cognitive coverage, more privacy. Worth tracking now even if the spec is not yet binding.
