For a year I've paid premium "think hard" rates to do throwaway work like linting alt-text. Opus 4.8 finally lets me stop.

It shipped yesterday with an effort dial: you tell Claude how hard to think, Low to Max, on every request. Same model, same price as 4.7. You're just deciding how much compute the task is worth.

That changes the math for accessibility work. Linting alt-text can run on Low. A full WCAG 2.2 reasoning pass across a component tree earns Max. I've wanted that split for ages, and I'm already rewiring my Bug Craft AI checks around it.

The benchmarks moved too (agentic coding went from 64.3% to 69.2%), but the dial is the part I'll use every day. Matching effort to task is how AI tooling stops being a flat tax on every job.

Where would you turn it up to Max, and where is Low good enough?
