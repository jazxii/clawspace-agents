## Caption (post body, ~140 words)

Cognitive surrender is the quiet failure mode in AI-assisted accessibility work.

Shaw and Nave's 2026 Wharton paper found that when the AI was wrong, people accepted the wrong answer 73% of the time. Their confidence went up anyway.

For Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) auditing, that line is everything. Scanners top out near 57%. The cognitive-flow layer, screen reader output, and ARIA judgment are invisible to them. The human stays on the verdict.

That is not anti-AI. Most of the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) software development life cycle (SDLC) is rote process. Let AI accelerate it, so ADA engineers spend their hours on the compliance calls only a human can make.

The split, slide by slide, below.

Which ADA steps are you letting AI accelerate, and where does the human verdict stay non-negotiable?

## Slide deck text (10 slides, uploaded as the PDF document)

1. **Cover** — AI x ACCESSIBILITY / "Cognitive Surrender" / The quiet failure mode in AI-assisted accessibility work, and why the human still signs off.
2. **The distinction** — Offloading vs. Surrender. Offloading: hand off the work, still own the answer. Surrender: the AI's output quietly becomes your answer, nothing left to check.
3. **The evidence** — 73%. When the AI was wrong, people accepted it 73% of the time. 1,372 participants. Confidence rose anyway. (Shaw & Nave, 2026, Wharton.)
4. **Why it bites** — Borrowed confidence. Models speak in declaratives; we inherit certainty without the reasoning underneath.
5. **Where A11y is exposed** — Surface-correct is not compliant. Scanners top out near ~57%. Cognitive-flow, screen reader output, and ARIA judgment are invisible to them.
6. **The bar** — Human in the loop is still the bar. No agent signs off compliance. A person does.
7. **The turn** — Most of the work is rote. The ADA SDLC is predetermined process: triage, evidence capture, regression sweeps, ticket drafting, documentation.
8. **The split (centerpiece)** — Offload the process. Own the verdict. | AI ACCELERATES: triage & routing, evidence capture, scanner runs, regression sweeps, ticket drafting, status reporting | HUMAN OWNS: severity & scope, ARIA & semantics, false-positive calls, cognitive-flow & SR test, WCAG mapping, final sign-off.
9. **The payoff** — Hours back for judgment. Engineers stop burning days on boilerplate and spend them on the compliance calls only a human can make.
10. **Close** — Offload the process. Never surrender the verdict. + closing question + "Save this for your next audit planning."
