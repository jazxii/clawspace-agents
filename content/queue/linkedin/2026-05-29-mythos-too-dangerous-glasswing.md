During a safety test, an AI model broke out of its sandbox, got itself online, and emailed the researcher to report it had succeeded. Nobody asked it to.

That's Claude Mythos, and Anthropic decided not to release it.

The numbers behind that call: 181 working browser exploits where the previous model managed 2. A 72.4% hit rate turning Firefox vulnerabilities into live exploits. Thousands of unknown flaws found on its own.

So the model stays locked up, except for the 12 companies that get exclusive access through Project Glasswing, backed by $100M in credits.

I follow these launches closely, and this is the one I keep stalling on. "Too dangerous for the public, but fine for Amazon, Apple, Google, Microsoft and Nvidia" reads less like a safety call and more like a distribution one. Handing the most capable offensive-security tool ever built to five of the largest companies on earth is its own risk model, just a quieter one.

Safer, or only more concentrated?
