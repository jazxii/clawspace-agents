The FTC just fined an AI accessibility overlay vendor $1 million for claiming it could deliver "WCAG 2.1 AA compliance in 48 hours." Final approval came through April 24, 2025. First federal enforcement action of its kind.

The vendor, accessiBe, was hit on three counts.

First: the 48-hour compliance claim. The FTC determined it was false, misleading, or unsubstantiated. Second: deceptive legal protection marketing. Hundreds of ADA suits were filed against accessiBe customers anyway, despite the promised shield. Third: undisclosed paid reviews presented as independent assessments.

For anyone in this space, this is a precedent worth memorising.

It establishes that "AI" plus "accessibility" plus "guaranteed compliance" is a marketing posture the FTC will now scrutinise directly. The agency did not need a new statute. Existing consumer protection law was enough once the gap between marketing copy and actual capability became measurable.

The 31% automated coverage ceiling that the W3C ACT Task Force publishes is the technical reason the 48-hour claim was indefensible. You cannot automate what is not automatically testable. Any vendor promising full WCAG compliance through a script tag is fighting that ceiling, not solving it.

So what does this mean for AI accessibility tooling? Audit your marketing copy. "Helps your team meet WCAG" is defensible. "Makes your site WCAG compliant" is now a litigation surface. The FTC has shown it will draw that line.

Are the AI tools your team uses making compliance claims they can actually substantiate?
