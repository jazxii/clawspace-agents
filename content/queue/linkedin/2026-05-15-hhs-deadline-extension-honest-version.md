HHS just extended the Section 504 web accessibility deadline by a year. The headline reads like a reprieve. The honest version: nothing about WCAG 2.1 AA changed. Only the date by which you have to be honest about it.

Larger recipients now have until May 11, 2027. Smaller recipients until May 10, 2028.

The extension is being read three different ways inside teams I've been talking to this week.

Read 1 (compliance lens): great, we get more time to pass the audit.

Read 2 (user lens): nothing changed for the assistive technology user who hit a barrier yesterday. The standard the deadline points to is unchanged.

Read 3 (shipping lens): we can move the work back a quarter and ship new features instead. We will get to remediation in Q3.

These three reads produce three different roadmaps. They are not the same goal.

The trouble starts when a team makes decisions that imply one read while saying out loud they are optimising for another. That gap is where accessibility work disappears.

Ask the team which read they are actually working from. And whether everyone on the team is working from the same one. That is a more useful question than whether the extension was the right call.

Compliance was always one possible answer. User experience was always a different one. Speed of shipping was always a third. None of those changed today. Only the date moved.

What read is your team working from this week?
