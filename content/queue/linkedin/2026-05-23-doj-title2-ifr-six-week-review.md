The DOJ Title II Interim Final Rule is six weeks in. Most of the teams I have talked to this month read the headline and concluded the work could slow down. The honest version is sharper than the headline.

The IFR moved exactly one thing. The WCAG 2.1 AA compliance date. State and local entities above 50,000 population now have until April 26, 2027. Smaller entities until April 26, 2028.

That is the entire change. The IFR did not suspend Title II's nondiscrimination obligation. It did not suspend the effective-communication obligation. Active complaints continue to move. Civil-rights advocacy groups are filing comments before the June 22 public comment window closes.

And the HHS healthcare deadline, the one tied to Section 504 funding, still hits on May 11, 2026. That deadline was not in the DOJ's IFR. It is a separate statute. Healthcare entities reading this week's Jackson Lewis or Duane Morris alert have already noticed.

So the calendar in front of a Non-Functional Testing (NFT) team running this work has three live dates, not one. The shipping conversation cannot collapse them into "we have another year."

The argument worth having on a roadmap call is not whether the extension was the right call. It is which obligation the team is actually optimising against. WCAG 2.1 AA conformance, the underlying nondiscrimination duty, or HHS-specific funding compliance.

Those three goals share a baseline but diverge in the work order. A team optimising for WCAG conformance ships axe-core in CI first. A team optimising for nondiscrimination ships assistive-tech user testing first. A team optimising for HHS ships before May 11.

If you work on a state/local web team, what are you telling product leadership about Q3 work this week?
