# DOJ Title II — one year more, same standard

**Format**: Single image, punchy caption. Hook lands before the "...more" cut at ~125 chars.

---

## Caption (front-loaded)

The DOJ pushed the ADA Title II deadline back a year. The standard did not move with it.

April 26, 2027 is the new date for state and local governments serving 50,000 or more people.

Smaller jurisdictions get until 2028.

WCAG 2.1 AA still applies. The clock is the only thing that changed.

I have read four threads this week framing this as a reprieve. It is not.

Users on screen readers, switch devices, and magnification are not waiting for April 2027. They are filing complaints right now.

Twelve more months until private lawsuits and DOJ administrative complaints become live exposure on the new clock.

Twelve more months to actually build the thing.

What I would do with the year:

Add an automated scanner to CI on day one.
Schedule a manual audit every quarter, screen reader and keyboard.
Track the conformance trajectory in something close to a VPAT, even if you are not procuring.

The teams that treat this as breathing room will use it.
The teams that treat it as a snooze button will be in the same place next April.

Which one is your team.

Save this for the next planning meeting where someone says we got the extension.
